# Unregulated but Generally Allowed Jurisdictions

## Alaska
**Status:** Unregulated But Generally Allowed

**Other Information:**
All major online fantasy sports service providers permit participation in Alaska. In February of 2022, HB 385 was introduced and referred to the Labor and Commerce Committee. If passed, the law enacted by HB 385 would authorize and impose a tax on mobile sports gaming and impose a tax on fantasy sports. Fantasy contests have never been challenged in the state. Alaska does not regulate fantasy operators in the state, so fantasy operators offer fantasy contests in Alaska without regulation.

## Florida
**Status:** Unregulated But Generally Allowed

**Other Information:**
Most major online fantasy sports service providers permit participation in Florida. Florida does not formally regulate fantasy contest operations in the state, so many fantasy operators offer fantasy contests in Florida without regulation. It is important to note that the D.C. Circuit Court of Appeals upheld a 2021 compact amendment between the Seminole Tribe and the State of Florida (the "Compact") that, among other things, allows the Seminole Tribe to offer certain fantasy sports contests. Florida does not currently have a separate law authorizing fantasy contests, but fantasy contests have been offered as skill games in the state for over a decade and many major fantasy contest operators continue to offer contests in Florida without issue.

## Illinois
**Status:** Unregulated But Generally Allowed

**Other Information:**
All major online fantasy sports service providers permit participation in Illinois. In 2020, the Illinois Supreme Court determined in Dew-Becker v. Wu, 2020 IL 124472 (April 16, 2020) that head-to-head fantasy sports is predominately skill-based and is therefore not gambling. Illinois does not formally regulate fantasy contest operations in the state, so fantasy operators offer fantasy contests in Illinois without regulation. SB2145 and SB 1224, which would create the "Fantasy Sports Consumer Protection Act" and allow for regulation of fantasy sports are currently being considered by the Illinois legislature (as of February 2025).

## Summary

This document lists 18 jurisdictions that are currently unregulated but generally allowed for fantasy sports operations. These states do not formally regulate fantasy contest operations, allowing major fantasy sports service providers to offer contests without specific licensing or regulatory requirements.

**Key Points:**
- All jurisdictions listed allow major fantasy sports operators to operate without formal regulation
- Several states have pending legislation that could introduce regulation in the future
- Most states classify fantasy sports as skill-based games rather than gambling
- Operators should monitor legislative developments in these jurisdictions for potential regulatory changes
