Understanding the graphics:
*The numbers shown are projections for the 2025 NFL regular season (Weeks 1-18).
*Some columns may not seem to be adding up correctly, but this is simply a product of rounding. The totals you see are correct.
*These are 17-game projections, which is why fringe players have little to no projection. Injuries will surely have an impact, so if you're comparing these to player props, be sure to factor in that risk. As a baseline, consider 
removing ~2 games worth of stats for QBs, WRs and TE, and ~3 games for RBs.
*Looking for sortable projections by position or category? Check out the projections tab inside the ESPN Fantasy game.
*'Team stat rankings' is where each team is projected to finish in the category that is shown.
*'Unit Grades' is not related to fantasy football and is an attempt at an objective ranking of each team at 10 key positions. The overall grades are weighted based on positional importance. The scale is 10 (best) to 1 (worst). A 
full rundown of Unit Grades can be found on page 61.
*'Strength of Schedule Ranking' is based on 2025 rosters (not last season's team record). '1' is easiest and '32' hardest. See the full list on page 60.
*Note that prior to the official release of the NFL schedule (generally mid May), the schedule shown includes the correct opponents, but the order is simulated.
*Have a question or notice an error? Contact Mike Clay on Twitter @MikeClayNFL
